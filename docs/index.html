Open P&L = base margin × (1 − hedge ratio) × 3.3M MMBtu cargo. "Long supply" = exposed to rising FOB cost (Brent or HH). "Short destination" = exposed to falling DES price (TTF or JKM).
Switch trigger = the JKM−TTF spread at which the alternative destination becomes more profitable than the default. Current JKM−TTF = $1.70/MMBtu. Ships 2, 5, 6 are all at or near their trigger thresholds.
Freight unhedged on all 6 vessels — OU σ=0.35 contributes ~8–15% of P&L variance as additional secondary exposure.
Reading the positions: The primary (locked) position captures the spread at time-zero benchmark prices and is protected from outright moves by the hedge. The secondary (open) position is where the portfolio makes or loses money as prices evolve — and for swing ships, where the two-stage re-routing generates the +$5.21M uplift. The switch triggers for Ships 2, 5, 6 are the key numbers to watch daily: current JKM−TTF of $1.70 sits above Ship 2's trigger ($1.01) and Ship 6's trigger ($0.96), meaning Asia is currently the better default — but the margin is thin for Ship 6.
