4 — Real options: Margrabe exchange option v7
Each FOB swing ship holds an option to exchange one destination margin for another. Valued via the Margrabe formula for exchange options:
V = Cargo × [ M₁ · N(d₁) − M₂ · N(d₂) ]
d₁ = [ ln(M₁/M₂) + ½σ²T ] / (σ√T)
d₂ = d₁ − σ√T
σ² = σ₁² + σ₂² − 2ρ·σ₁·σ₂   (spread vol)

Individual ship contribution to portfolio variance

★ Bottom line: The portfolio manages $84.30M of expected margin (sum-of-parts) but its actual risk profile is far superior to six independently-managed ships. The $4.74M std dev reduction and $17.86M VaR improvement come from HH–Brent decorrelation (ρ=0.15/0.05) — the fleet’s structural diversifier. v7 upgrade Regime-switching amplifies this: in stress, TTF–JKM drops to 0.40 making swing ships more valuable (+55% option value), partially offsetting the wider tail. The +$6.41M two-stage routing and +$4.66M CVaR uplift require active management (JKM–TTF spread vs trigger thresholds daily). Total: portfolio earns $10.45M more E[P&L] with $19.10M better CVaR than a naïve sum.

Key merger conclusions
CVaR improves by $7.86M — the biggest single benefit. Both C2 vessels now loading from US Gulf (HH-linked), adding a second uncorrelated HH-origin pair alongside Ship 1 & 6, amplifying tail diversification.

Two-stage uplift increases by $3.55M — 5 swing ships (vs 3) with C2 vessels on US→EU default. US→Asia switch available at 52d RT (lower $/day but strong JKM arbitrage in stress).

Std Dev narrows by $2.57M — C2 vessels now HH-linked (same as Ships 1 & 6), slightly reducing the cross-origin diversification vs ME scenario. Net variance still improved vs simple sum.

HH concentration increases — C2 vessels now HH-linked alongside Ships 1 & 6. If HH spikes, 4 of 8 ships face supply cost pressure simultaneously. Partial offset: stronger US→EU margins when HH is low.

Net value of merger: +$7.86M CVaR uplift + $3.55M two-stage uplift = strong case for acquisition/charter. AIS-updated March 2026: C2 vessels repositioned to US Gulf.
